# Concept Review

# INTRODUCTION

We have many festivals in India. Festivals and occasions are related to religious beliefs, history, culture, etc. They bring us joy, love and happiness. We all get together to celebrate them.

![](images/35c69b85e463ff976c85accc412fc5f0beb214de5c3a70d061783ed9a2279b04.jpg)

# Types of Festivals

There are three types of festivals celebrated in India - National Festivals, Religious Festivals and Harvesting Festivals.

1. National Festivals: National festivals are special days that are celebrated across the whole country. National holidays are observed on the day of national festivals. The three main national festivals of India are:

$\checkmark$  Independence Day

On  $15^{\text{th}}$  August 1947, when India got freedom from British rule.  
We celebrate Independence Day every year on 15 August.

![](images/a64270dc686c01c282d67ad1771acc4b3cc407092837c7977db65a2c51f34432.jpg)

- On this day, the Prime Minister of India hoists the National flag at Red Fort in Delhi.

![](images/e2049f90bef0b31b4caded7db96b833eab79daace7b10287cb286a92d5e581ca.jpg)  
Red fort

# Republic Day

Republic Day is celebrated on  $26^{\text{th}}$  January.  
Every year on this day, the President of India unfurls the flag at Rajpath.  
A grand parade is organised at the India Gate in New Delhi.  
The President takes the salute of the parade.

![](images/b5308afae8e7904a87987f800d8890e8b5c87fafe4c76c2590d4964176983ff9.jpg)

# Gandhi Jayanti

Mahatma Gandhi was born on  $2^{\text{nd}}$  October, 1869.  
He is called the 'Father of the Nation'.  
Every year, 2nd of October is celebrated as Gandhi Jayanti.  
Raj Ghat is a memorial place of Mahatma Gandhi.

# 2. Religious Festivals: Some of the most popular religious festivals of India are:

# Diwali

It is a festival of light.  
It is celebrated to honour Lord Rama. It is believed that on this day,

Lord Rama returned to Ayodhya with his wife and brother, after 14 years of exile.

Lakshmi puja is done on this day.  
- People decorate their homes with earthen lamps, candles and lights.  
They enjoy bursting cracker and exchanging sweets and gifts with their friends and relatives.

![](images/63abaed311ba118becce674de46ef7e8d3bf5995497d79a0e10bc30b704c558a.jpg)  
Diwali

# Holi

It is a festival of colours.  
- On the eve of the Holi festival, people light a bonfire. It is celebrated by the name of Holika Dahan or Chhoti Holi.  
- On the day of the festival, people greet each other and apply dry 'gulal' and wet colours on each other.  
Children play with 'pichkari' and water balloons.

![](images/eefbe9ada73770692ad6a9a9df1b0e604102d958b64878a08802c6a2390d8bac.jpg)  
Holi

# ✓ Dussehra

Dussehra marks the end of Navratri.  
It is also known as Vijayadashami.  
People celebrate the victory of Lord Ram over Raavan on this day.  
The enactments of Ramleela are organised in many places.  
- On this day, effigies of Ravan, Kumbhkaran and Meghnad are burnt by the people.

![](images/495f910f7ea27853ee3f3b494571fc7952eacbdd312d4288c8a8137f39da003c.jpg)  
Dussehra: Effigies of Kumbhkaran, Ravan and Meghnad

# Eid

Eid is celebrated by Muslims.  
- On this day, people offer morning prayers known as 'namaz' in the mosque.  
They wish each other 'Eid-Mubarak'.  
- 'Meethi seviyan' is prepared and served to family and guests.

![](images/83276f46817d5a41f82cf31fd4aa8bb782dd14a2dec125cc931bef8d45b28527.jpg)  
Eid

# ✓ Guruparv (Gurpurab)

Every year, it is celebrated on Guru Nanak Dev Ji's birthday.  
Guruparv is celebrated by Sikhs.  
People go to Gurudwaras to pray.  
Free food called 'langar' is served to all visitors in Gurudwaras.

# Christmas

- Christmas is celebrated on the 25th of December every year.  
- On this day, Christians celebrate the birthday of Lord Jesus Christ.  
They go to Church to offer prayers.  
- People decorate Christmas trees and give gifts to each other.

![](images/379064df085a62c0066baba9d770cca9aba0299a12c546e3ca790ba76905f454.jpg)

# 3. Harvesting Festivals: The harvesting festival is an annual celebration that occurs around the time of the harvesting of main crops in a given region. The most popular harvesting festivals in India are:

# ✓ Makar Sankranti

It is celebrated in the month of January.  
This festival is dedicated to the Hindu sun God 'Surya'.  
- Kite flying is a tradition of Makar Sankranti so it is also called the 'Kite Flying Festival'.

![](images/52520d36ffda09bb28cbc70c505deadd3e98521dbca49e113f35ec57dcc21e4c.jpg)  
Kite flying on Makar Sankranti

# Lohri

- Lohri is a harvest festival in the state of Punjab.  
It is celebrated in the month of January.  
- On this day, family members and friends gather around bonfires and sing together.

![](images/aad39e9d671e11b3d05e84afebc687c18edec6f1543db1e9c4cab1f15a6397ef.jpg)  
Lohri

# Onam

Onam is the harvest festival of Kerala.  
A boat race is conducted during the festival of Onam.  
- Colourful arrangement of flowers are created during the ten-day celebration.

![](images/9340cb0fb8153f518f44a63e8dc135c35faba907121b1d94479ebee3e926f859.jpg)  
Onam : Boat race

- Apart from these, the other famous harvesting festivals of India are – Baisakhi, Basant Panchmi and Pongal.
